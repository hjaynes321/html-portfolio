The Best Movies According to Holly

My Top Three Movies of All Time

Groundhog Day

Groundhog Day is in fact ground-breaking for what it accomplished. One man relives the same day over and over until he becomes a better person. Seemingly a comedy at first watch, one realizes through repetition (much like the main character) that there is much more to be gleaned through each watch, showing a more dramatic side of the film.

The Princess Bride

The Princess Bride is the story of true love told through a child's book, while also brought to life by dynamic characters who exhibit the emotions of love lost and found. The movie is an adventure, comedy, drama, and also has elements of terror and suspense. The ultimate message is clear, though: live your life to love.

Dumb and Dumber

Although Dumb and Dumber is not a critically acclaimed movie and deemed by many to be too inane to receive any true accolades, it is still in fact an amusing movie that always makes me laugh. This may be my real "guilty pleasure" of a movie, but I stand by the comedic genius of Jim Carrey.
